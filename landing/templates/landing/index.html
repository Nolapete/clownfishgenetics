Clownfish Species

  • Amphiprion ocellaris: Common Clownfish, False Percula Clownfish, Ocellaris Clownfish
  • Amphiprion percula: True Percula Clownfish, Clown Anemonefish
  • Amphiprion frenatus: Tomato Clownfish, Fire Clownfish
  • Amphiprion polymnus: Saddleback Clownfish, Red Saddleback Clownfish
  • Amphiprion bicinctus: Two-Banded Clownfish, Red Sea Clownfish
  • Amphiprion chrysopterus: Orange-Fin Anemonefish
  • Amphiprion clarkii: Clark's Anemonefish, Yellowtail Clownfish
  • Amphiprion sandaracinos: Orange Skunk Clownfish

Designer Clownfish Variants

  • Naked Clownfish: Lack the typical orange coloration, often with a transparent appearance.
  • Platinum Clownfish: Silver or platinum-colored clownfish, lacking the typical orange or black markings.
  • Black Ice Clownfish: Black and white coloration, resembling the appearance of cracked ice.
  • Frostbite Clownfish: A variant with icy blue and white colors.
  • Midnight Clownfish: Dark-colored clownfish, often with black or deep blue hues.
  • Snowflake Clownfish: White or light-colored clownfish with irregular black markings.
  • Mocha Storm Clownfish: Unique brown and white coloration, resembling a stormy pattern.
  • DaVinci Clownfish: Characterized by intricate and artistic patterns on the body.
  • Tangerine Clownfish: Intense orange coloration.
  • Blue Moon Clownfish: Blue and black coloration with a moon-like appearance.
  • Lightning Maroon Clownfish: A maroon clownfish variant with lightning-like patterns.
  • Wyoming White Clownfish: Selectively bred variant with reduced or absent orange pigmentation.
  • Golden Nugget Clownfish: Yellow or gold-colored clownfish.
  • Phantom Clownfish: A variant with a reduced or absent body stripe.
  • Caramel Clownfish: Brown or caramel-colored clownfish.
  • Pink Skunk Clownfish: A variant with pink coloration instead of the typical white.
  • Gladiator Clownfish: Characterized by extended and exaggerated fins.
  • Barcode Clownfish: Distinctive striping pattern resembling a barcode.
  • Domino Clownfish: Black and white pattern resembling dominoes.
  • Extreme Misbar Clownfish: Clownfish with irregular and extreme misbar patterns.
